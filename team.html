Who We Are

The Research Team

The StaMPS Data Framework is led at McGill University and the Lady Davis Institute for Medical Research, in collaboration with the Psychedelic Mental Health Access Alliance — and shaped by an international panel of 78 experts.

Principal Investigator

Kyle T. Greenway, M.D., M.Sc., FRCPC

Principal Investigator — McGill University

Dr. Greenway is an Assistant Professor in the Department of Psychiatry at McGill University, an attending psychiatrist at the Jewish General Hospital in Montreal, and an investigator at the Lady Davis Institute for Medical Research. He directs the Jewish General Hospital's ketamine-assisted therapy program — among the few clinics worldwide to offer a psychedelic-based therapy for severe depression within a public payer system — and co-leads a research laboratory focused on psychedelic and other novel psychiatric interventions.

His research spans clinical, naturalistic, and fundamental studies of psychedelic-assisted therapies, with particular attention to the extra-pharmacological factors — set, setting, culture, and care context — that shape treatment outcomes. He completed postdoctoral studies at the Imperial College London Centre for Psychedelic Research and the McGill Clinical Investigator Program. His FRQS Chercheur-Boursier clinical research scholar award supports this program of work.

Collaboration with the Psychedelic Mental Health Access Alliance

A partnership for equitable, evidence-based implementation The Psychedelic Mental Health Access Alliance (PMHA Alliance) is a key sponsor and collaborator of this project. Founded in 2023, the PMHA Alliance works to expand access to psychedelic-assisted therapy for people most affected by trauma, poverty, and unmet mental health needs — generating the evidence, implementation learnings, and practical tools needed to responsibly integrate these therapies into publicly funded healthcare systems, including Medicaid-fundable models of care. Standardized, purpose-sensitive data collection is foundational to that mission: it is what allows real-world programs, trials, and payers to speak a common language about safety, effectiveness, and value.

Funding & Support

This study (protocol 25-05-146-01) is supported by the Psychedelic Mental Health Access Alliance (PMHA), the Fonds de recherche du Québec — Santé (FRQS) Chercheur-Boursier J1 award, and the Lady Davis Institute for Medical Research (LDI).

The Expert Panel

The framework's substance comes from an international panel of 78 experts across 13 countries — clinicians, researchers, policy and health-economics specialists, and others with deep experience in psychedelic medicine. We are grateful for their continued, thoughtful participation across rounds.
